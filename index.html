Aarhus, Denmark

'Magic Mushrooms'

In a public parking garage in Aarhus, Denmark, a country where skyscrapers still are rather uncommon, Danish-Norwegian artists Michael Elmgreen and Ingar Dragset have installed Magic Mushrooms, an inverted fictitious high-rise metropolis model. Elmgreen has also described the artwork as “revenge” for not being allowed to have a doll house to play with when he was a little boy.

Frights and Delights

Jersey Shore Hauntings

In the late 1970s and early 1980s, if you wanted to be scared out of your skin outside of Halloween, the place to be was the Jersey Shore, where haunted attractions dotted the boardwalk. These purpose-built, walk-through “houses of horror” were staffed in large part by student actors who loved theatrics and makeup, knew how to improvise, and operated under laws that were a lot less strict than you might expect. So as you might imagine, these places soon became legendary.

Signal Mountain, Tennessee

Spaceship House

By 1972, Star Trek had come and gone and the possibility of alien life was fully lodged into the cultural consciousness. Curtis King embraced this extraterrestrial vibe and built the Spaceship House for his son in 1972, tucking it away in the woods near Signal Mountain, Tennessee. Complete with a drop-down staircase to enter the elevated spacecraft, the house had every detail to channel futuristic living and only cost $250,000 to build.

Culinary Traditions

Thailand’s Cannabis Cuisine

From 1934 to 2018, marijuana was illegal in Thailand, even though the use of marijuana in Thai food was not an uncommon sight. “Tender ganja leaves,” for example, are called for in an eel-curry recipe in Mae Khrua Hua Pa, generally considered Thailand’s oldest cookbook, first printed in 1908. But it’s likely that the use of the plant goes back much, much earlier, with origins in medicine rather than food.

Montserrat

AIR Studio Ruins

You can’t tell by looking at it now, but this grimy complex was once a hot spot for the musical greats of the 1980s. Opened by George Martin, the famous English record producer and musician who was sometimes called the “fifth Beatle,” the studio hosted legends like the Rolling Stones, Elton John, and Stevie Wonder within its state-of-the-art walls. But only a decade after the celebrated studio opened, its life soon came to an abrupt end.

Tying Mythic Knots

Knot-tying is an ancient art and technology believed to predate the axe and even the wheel. Shipped around the world, the techniques and legends accompanying each knot can tell a story of geography, of travel, and of the cultures where they landed. By the end of our time together, you'll not only have practiced tying knots that people have used for thousands of years, but you'll also understand the ways we make meaning through myth and story today.
